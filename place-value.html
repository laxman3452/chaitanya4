Day 7: Number Sense & Seven-Digit Place Value

Part I: Historical Context

Before Modern Numbers: Early humans used 1-to-1 correspondence to count items like livestock or family members. They used:

  • Tally marks or notches cut into wood/bones.
  • Physical objects like pebbles or shells.
  • Knots tied into ropes.

The Breakthrough: The Hindu-Arabic (Decimal) system replaced physical objects with just 10 simple digits (0-9) organized by positional values!

Teacher Tools: Quick Assessment Correction Key

  1. Q1 Answer: In 18,79,312, the digit 8 is in the Lakhs place. Its place value is 8,00,000.
  2. Q2 Answer: In 93,45,281, the digit 5 holds the Thousands place, and its value is 5,000.
  3. Q3 Answer: 8,00,201 = (8 × 1,00,000) + (0 × 10,000) + (0 × 1,000) + (2 × 100) + (0 × 10) + (1 × 1)
  4. Q4 Answer: Standard compiled form value is 5,32,694.
